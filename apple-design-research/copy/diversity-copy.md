## Key Initiatives

### 1. Inclusive Product Design
- **Focus:** Designing accessibility into products from the beginning
- **Example:** Face ID development included diverse facial feature variations and coverings to serve users of all skin tones, ages, and vision abilities
- **Quote:** "Inclusion is not something you can tack on at the end...it's an ongoing effort" — Jerremy Holland, AI Research

### 2. Veterans Community Support
- Comprehensive health, financial, and wellness benefits tailored for Veterans
- Veterans@Apple network provides peer support and advocacy
- Benefits education ensures awareness of available resources
- **Quote:** "Just letting people know what we offer helps bring about dialogue" — Mike Burtnick, AppleCare Manager

### 3. HBCU Engineering Pathways
- **New Silicon Initiative** partners with Historically Black Colleges and Universities
- Institutions include Howard University, Morgan State, North Carolina A&T, and others
- Hands-on learning, guest lectures, and one-on-one mentoring from Apple engineers

### 4. Pay Equity Commitment
- Gender pay equity achieved and maintained globally since 2017
- Race and ethnicity pay equity achieved in the United States
- Annual third-party audits ensure ongoing compliance

### 5. Accessibility Features
- **SignTime:** Remote sign language interpretation for customers and employees
- Supports ASL (US/Canada), BSL (UK), LSF (France), HandTime (Japan), SignChat (Germany)
- Available via web browsers and in Apple Store locations
- **Quote:** "This made it easier to build relationships, removed unnecessary barriers" — Ricky Winarta, Apple

### 6. Diversity Network Associations (DNAs)
- Volunteer-led employee groups celebrating global communities
- Open to all employees regardless of background
- Foster belonging through cultural recognition and engagement events
